NBA Player Shot Charts

Amaan Bhojani, Sunny Kang, Johnson Wang, Abyan Nawaz, Matthew Vieira

Course Project as part of DS4200 S22: Information Visualization, taught by Prof. Ab Mosca, Data Visualization @ Khoury, Northeastern University.

Abstract

Data driven approaches in the realm of basketball to improve team performance are not centralized or standardized. With the use of short charts and heat maps, teams can better understand their chemistry, efficiency and productivity. The visualizations provided show overlapping heat maps where certain players perform better than others. To better structure your team with the nuance of data, coaches and staff can utilize the visualizations to know what players perform better with certain teammates, against certain players/teams, in particular quarters of the game.

Visualization Explanation

Current visualization explanation: The scatter plot shows EFG% (umbrella stat that tells how effective a team is) versus the teams wins. Here we can see data that is positively correlated which will tell our end users a sector to improve on. In the bargraph we simply have the wins each team had in the season, once brushing and linking is created, teams can easily see how wins and effective field goal percentage (EFG) are correlated.
